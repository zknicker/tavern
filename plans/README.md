## Direction findings (options, not ranked against fixes)

- **Runtime credentials / pairing** — partially addressed by plan 003.
  `docs/api/auth.md` promises "explicit Tavern-issued credentials" for
  external clients; after 003 the natural next step is a pairing UX (token
  paste/QR in settings) and per-client tokens.
- **Multi-agent productization** — `docs/features/agents.md` explicitly
  frames today's scope as the "current single-agent product boundary," yet
  the plumbing already exists: a `subAgent.list` tRPC surface
  (`apps/server/src/api/sub-agent/`), a workers API, and `specs/workers.md` /
  `specs/agent-runtimes/`. The data model (participants, per-agent sessions)
  was built multi-agent-shaped. Cheap to spike: a design doc on what breaks
  in the UI when two agents share a chat.
- **External messaging channels (Discord)** — half-built intent throughout:
  `DISCORD_BOT_TOKEN` / `DISCORD_APPLICATION_ID` in `.env.example` ("Runtime
  Discord channel support"), `apps/server/src/messaging-platform/service.ts`,
  Discord presentation logic in `apps/server/src/chat/list.ts`, and the dead
  messenger package (removed by plan 005). Either finish the channel story or
  excise the presentation stubs; the half-state costs maintenance on every
  chat-list change.
